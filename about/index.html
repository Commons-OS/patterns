What is a Commons?

"A commons is a shared resource ‚Äî whether natural, cultural, digital, or social ‚Äî that is governed by a community according to rules and norms that ensure its sustainability and equitable access for all."

‚Äî Inspired by the work of Elinor Ostrom, Nobel Prize in Economics 2009

Commons have existed throughout human history: shared pastures, fisheries, forests, irrigation systems, and knowledge traditions. They represent a third way beyond the false dichotomy of state control versus private ownership ‚Äî a way where communities self-organize to manage shared resources sustainably.

Today, we see commons everywhere: open-source software (Linux, Wikipedia), community land trusts, cooperative businesses (Mondragon, REI), urban commons (community gardens, makerspaces), and knowledge commons (Creative Commons, open science). The commons is not a relic of the past ‚Äî it's a blueprint for the future.
